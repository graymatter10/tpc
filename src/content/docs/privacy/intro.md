## What is Privacy

Privacy means control over your personal data. It defines how your information is
collected, used, and shared.
Key points:

- Protects personal identity
- Limits data exposure
- Reduces risk of misuse

## Types of Personal Data

Your data includes:

- Name, phone number, email
- Location data
- Browsing history
- Financial information
- Photos and messages

## Why Privacy Matters

Loss of privacy leads to real risks.
Risks:

- Identity theft
- Financial fraud
- Targeted scams
- Tracking and profiling


## Data Collection

Websites and apps collect your data.
Common methods:

- Cookies
- Tracking scripts
- Account registration
- App permissions

## Protect Your Privacy

### Strong Account Security

- Use strong, unique passwords
- Enable two factor authentication

### Control App Permissions

- Allow only needed access
- Disable camera, mic, location when not required

### Safe Browsing

- Use trusted websites
- Avoid unknown downloads
- Clear cookies regularly

### Use Privacy Tools

- VPN to hide your IP
- Privacy focused browsers
- Ad blockers


## Social Media Privacy

You must control what you share.
Steps:

- Set profile to private
- Limit personal details
- Review posts before sharing
- Check privacy settings regularly

## Public WiFi Safety

Public networks expose your data.
Steps:

- Avoid sensitive tasks
- Use VPN
- Turn off auto connect

## Data Backup and Deletion

Manage your stored data.

- Backup important files
- Delete unused accounts
- Remove old data

## Conclusion

Privacy needs active control. Follow these steps to protect your personal data
online.
